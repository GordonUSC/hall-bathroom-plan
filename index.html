Protect the centerpiece

Reglazing, done right

The reglaze is the make-or-break. Go pro on the tub and any in-shower wet wall (DIY epoxy fails most in wet zones). Then baby it for 30 days. That is the difference between a 5-year finish and a 15-year one.

Do

  • Hire a real pro for the tub + wet walls (Miracle Method and LA refinishers, ~$1,100–2,300 for both)
  • Use only non-abrasive, non-acidic cleaners (mild dish soap, a soft sponge)
  • Wipe surfaces dry after use
  • Treat a chip fast, water under a chip is what causes peeling

Don't

  • No suction-cup bath mats, ever (the #1 cause of peeling)
  • No bleach, ammonia, abrasive pads, or powder cleansers
  • Don't use the tub hard for ~30 days while it fully cures
  • Don't cheap out with a Groupon refinisher on the one irreversible step

One honest pause, from someone who cares about your house

Going white is the one permanent decision here. That pink is a real mid-century time capsule, and there's a whole movement (Save the Pink Bathrooms, since 2009) of people who restore it instead, especially in design-forward LA pockets where an intact period-pink bath is an asset. You already chose white, and white is going to be beautiful. I just wanted you to have made the call on purpose. Either way, I'm with you.
