Protect the centerpiece

Reglazing, done right

The reglaze is make-or-break. Go pro on the tub and any in-shower wet wall (DIY epoxy fails most in wet zones), then baby it 30 days. That's a 5-year finish versus a 15-year one.

Do

  • Hire a real pro for tub + wet walls (~$1,100–2,300 for both)
  • Non-abrasive, non-acidic cleaners only (mild dish soap, soft sponge)
  • Wipe surfaces dry after use
  • Fix any chip fast, water under a chip is what peels it

Don't

  • No suction-cup bath mats, ever (the #1 cause of peeling)
  • No bleach, ammonia, abrasive pads, or powder cleansers
  • Don't use the tub hard for ~30 days while it cures
  • Don't cheap out on the one step you can't undo

Before you hire

Questions to ask

Bring these to each quote. Good answers separate a pro from a Groupon.

🛁 The reglaze pro

  • What prep and bonding agent do you use on already-glazed tile?
  • What's the warranty, and what voids it?
  • How soon can I use it, and what's full cure time?
  • Can you match one uniform white across the tub and the walls?
  • How long do the fumes last?

🔧 The plumber

  • Can you relocate supply + drain for a wall-mount vanity?
  • Can you set the new toilet on the same trip?
  • Any likely surprises behind this old wall?
  • Flat rate or hourly?

⚡ The electrician

  • Can the new fan reuse the existing box and circuit?
  • Can you wire two sconces on a dimmer?
  • Is the GFCI up to current code?
  • Can you combine with the plumber's visit?
Keep it beautiful

The first month matters

A reglaze rewards patience. Treat it right early and it lasts 15+ years.

First 24 hours
Don't touch it. No water, no items, nothing on the surface while it sets.
First 30 days
Gentle use only. No suction mats, no harsh cleaners. It's curing to full hardness.
Forever after
Soft sponge + mild dish soap, wipe dry, fix any chip fast. That's the whole secret.
